Bring your used tech (and more!) to RobinHood and we’ll either put it to good use or ensure it is recycled responsibly. It costs us money to recycle certain non-functioning items such as keyboards and monitors. If you know your donation cannot be repaired, please consider making a financial donation to cover recycling costs or consider taking them to the Center for Hard to Recycle Materials instead. Whatever you do, please do not take ewaste to a landfill!

How to donate

Donations may be taken to our workspace on Sunday from 1-5pm, Wednesday from 6-8pm, Friday from 3-7pm, and Saturday from 9am-1pm. 
For the sake of time it is suggested that you organize or sort your donations in a manner that makes categorization easy. 
If you are wanting a tax receipt, it is advised to have a written list of the items in question that are being donated; this will make your donation time quicker and will help our volunteers efficiently sort items. 
If you represent an organization or business with a large amount of hardware to donate, please email us to arrange a pick-up or drop-off. Keep in mind that we are an all-volunteer run organization, so our ability to pick up equipment varies.
Please Signup/Login to the website and create a donation entry from your panel, so that it will be easy for us to create donation entry.

Your personal data is secure

When you donate your computer to Free I.T. Athens, our volunteers remove its hard drive and put the drive through a data wiping process using free software called DBAN. DBAN removes data by writing random numbers to the disk. After your data has been wiped from the hard drive volunteers will refurbish your computer using a free operating system called GNU/Linux. Your computer will either be granted to a volunteer for completing community service, sold to an individual or family that cannot afford a new computer, or granted to a non-profit organization/community group. If your computer cannot be refurbished, it will be recycled through a certified recycler. Don’t donate to those other guys, they could be shipping e-waste overseas to be dumped!
